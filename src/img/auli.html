Settled at an altitude of 3519 metres, Gorson Bugyal is a popular skiing destination that is full of picturesque vistas. About 19 km from Joshimath, this Himalayan alpine meadow is one of the well-maintained trek routes. During the spring season, the place is all over the lush green grass, making it ideal for camping as well. Trek to Gorson Bugyal passes through lush flora of apple orchards and deodar & oak forests.

Joshimath

Like several other spectacular places in Auli, Joshimath offers gorgeous views of snow-covered Himalayan peaks and serve as a prominent site for adventure junkies and travellers who are on their spiritual quest.

Rudraprayag

Located at the elevation of 2936 ft, Rudraprayag is a beautiful destination that holds great importance among Hindu pilgrims. Being one of the five confluences of Alaknanda, Rudraprayag serves as a point of confluence of rivers Mandakini and Alaknanda. Besides, itâ€™s a place where Jim Corbett hunted a man-eating leopard. Things to Do in Auli

Hiking & Trekking

When it comes to hiking & trekking, you have options like the Auli-Gorson trek, Kuari Pass Trek, and a hike to Kwani Bugyal. Talking about the Auli-Gorson trek, it is one of the most delightful and adventurous treks from Auli to Gorson Bugyal as the route is filled with charming views that enhance the trekking experience. The famous Kuari Pass has been attracting backpackers and trekkers from far and wide for decades. The trek starts in Joshimath and offers a brief stopover at Devaprayag and is full of adventures and picturesque landscapes, comprising alpine meadows and mighty mountains laden with snow. Speaking of the hike to Kwani Bugyal, it isnâ€™t that challenging for first-timers, and the route is filled with mesmerising views of green pastures and tall oak trees that are a true feast to eyes and soul.

Ropeway Ride

Other than skiing and hiking, get the extra dose of thrill with a ropeway ride. It is one of the thrilling activities to do in Auli. Enjoy the astonishing views of Sanjivani Parvat and Nanda Devi Peak with the cable car ride from Auli GMVN Clifftop to Joshimath. Shopping at the Local Market

Since Auli is a skiing destination, choices for shopping other than ski equipment are modest. So, if you want to go on a shopping spree, you can head to the market of Joshimath. It is just 11 km away from Auli but has many shops selling a wide variety of products including shawls, artefacts, woollens, and souvenirs.

Enjoy Authentic Garhwali Cuisine

Auli is known for its delectable Garhwali cuisine. So, when you are here, do explore the food culture of Garhwal. At local restaurants, you will find a variety of leafy vegetables, dals, and rice that dominate the menu. In case you want to relish the traditional meat, try Kachmouli, a smoked, goat delicacy that is made by marinating and roasting the meat over green twigs. In desserts, you can try Bal Mithai, which is a famous local sweet, Singhori, and more.
